Although I have been taking computer science classes on and off for almost 25 years (!!!), one thing I had never done until this week was pair program.  I can remember maybe one class ever where I even was supposed to work with a partner -- it was Computational Physics, and we were working on some kind of graphical representation of a wavefunction.  It wasn't quite what I would call pair programing -- as I recall, we split the work up between us.  This is one of many reasons why I'm so happy to be learing with Flatiron -- in addition to learning modern frameworks, I am also learning modern industry practices.

For the last almost two months, I have been slowly plowing through the Object-Oriented Ruby unit of Flatiron's curriculum.  It had definitely not been my plan to spend so much time on this one unit, but I have been learning a lot of new things along the way.  I was finally almost finished, except for the final project and the OO Tic-Tac-Toe lab, which is supposed to be done with a partner or group.  I made plans to pair program with somebody, but the plan fell through.  Luckily, I found a new partner who was awesome!

We decided that I would fork the project and share my screen through Zoom.  I imagine watching me type was pretty frustrating for my partner, because so many times I was able to just point to places in the code with my cursor, or type out a line of code because it was a better explanation than what I was saying -- and she had to communicate completely verbally.  Between the two of us, we were able to get through the tests.  There were a few places that we had errors for quite a while, but I think our work schedules helped with this -- we really couldn't meet longer than about an hour and a half, so we were able to walk away from the errors and issues and come back to it after a day or two with fresh minds.

Ultimately we created a fully functional tic-tac-toe that allows humans to play each other or against the computer.  We were both very happy we got it done!  Next time I will probably be the one looking at the shared screen as my partner types, so that will certainly be an exercise in patience.  I am up for the challenge, though!
